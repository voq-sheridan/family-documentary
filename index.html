OUR JOURNEY

A Family's Immigration Story to Canada

01

The Beginning — Our Parents

In 1998, our parents made the courageous decision to leave everything familiar behind. With two young children and dreams of a better future, they embarked on a journey across continents, carrying nothing but hope and determination.

Arrival day, 1998 — A new beginning

LISTEN — Mother's Story

The first winter was the hardest. The snow, the cold, the unfamiliar faces. But slowly, this foreign land began to feel like home. Small victories became celebrations — the first job, the first friend, the first "thank you" in perfect English.

"We came here with nothing but dreams. Now, we have a home, a future, and a chance to give our children what we never had."

02

Between Two Worlds — Older Brother

I was seven when we arrived. Old enough to remember the country we left, young enough to adapt. The playground was where I learned English, where accents faded and friendships formed over games of hockey on frozen ponds.

First day of school — Grade 2

LISTEN — Growing Up Canadian

At home, we spoke our mother tongue. At school, I was just another Canadian kid. This duality became my strength — bridging cultures, carrying two identities with equal pride. I learned that home isn't just a place; it's wherever your people are.

03

Born Here, Rooted There — Younger Brother

I was born just two years after we arrived. Canada is all I've ever known, yet I grew up surrounded by stories of another place. My parents' homeland lives in our traditions, our food, the language we speak at dinner tables.

Family traditions — Holiday celebration

LISTEN — Finding Identity

I'm Canadian through and through, but my heritage is woven into who I am. I've learned that you can honor where your family came from while fully embracing where you are. Identity isn't about choosing one over the other — it's about celebrating both.

"I may not remember the journey, but I carry it with me every day. This is what it means to be the child of immigrants — to be the bridge between two worlds."

This is our story — one family among millions who have woven their threads into the fabric of Canada. We came as strangers and became neighbors, friends, citizens.

Immigration isn't just about crossing borders. It's about courage, sacrifice, and the unshakeable belief in a better tomorrow.

This is where we belong now. This is home.
